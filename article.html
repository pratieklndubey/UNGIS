Twenty-second Meeting of the United Nations Group on the Information Society

Held during the WSIS+20 High-Level Event, this session brought together heads of agencies to coordinate ensuring the UN system’s contribution to the implementation of the WSIS outcomes remains relevant and impactful.

Overview

The Twenty-second meeting of UNGIS served as a critical platform to align the group’s activities with the WSIS+20 Review process and the broader Pact for the Future. Member agencies discussed the evolving digital landscape, emphasizing the need for strengthened inter-agency cooperation to address emerging challenges such as AI governance and the digital divide.

Key Outcomes

  • Joint Statement: Adoption of a unified UNGIS contribution to the Global Digital Compact.
  • Work Plan 2025-2026: Agreement on a roadmap focusing on capacity building and regional implementation.
  • WSIS+20 Review: Consensus on the "Elements Paper" submission, highlighting twenty years of progress and future directions.

High-Level Segment

The High-Level segment featured interventions from the Secretary-General of ITU, the Secretary-General of UNCTAD, and the Administrator of UNDP. The leaders reaffirmed their commitment to utilizing digital technologies as an accelerator for the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

"UNGIS remains the central pillar for UN coordination on digital issues, ensuring that no one is left behind in the information society."

Next Steps

The group agreed to convene virtually in November 2025 to finalize the inputs for the 2026 UN General Assembly review. The rotating chairmanship will pass to UNESCO in the next cycle.

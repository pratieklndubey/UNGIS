United Nations Group on the
Information Society

The inter-agency mechanism for coordinating the implementation of the World Summit on the Information Society (WSIS) outcomes.

The United Nations Group on the Information Society (UNGIS) serves as the primary inter-agency mechanism within the UN system to coordinate substantive policy issues related to the implementation of the outcomes of the World Summit on the Information Society (WSIS).

Our Mandate

Established in 2006 effectively after the WSIS Tunis phase, UNGIS was endorsed by the UN Chief Executives Board (CEB) with a mandate to promote policy coherence and program coordination concerning Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs) across the UN system.

Global Impact

UNGIS works to ensure that science and technology, particularly ICTs for development, remain prominent on the UN Agenda, aligning digital strategies with internationally agreed development goals, including the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

Key Objectives

  • Mainstreaming ICTs

    Integrating the Information Society Agenda into the activities and programs of CEB members to ensure digital tools drive development.

  • Coordinating Strategies

    Facilitating synergies among United Nations organizations to maximize joint efforts, avoid duplication, and enhance effectiveness.

  • Promoting Access

    Facilitating developing countries' access to new and emerging technologies and promoting public awareness of UN system implementation efforts.
